What Is Land Degradation?

Land degradation refers to the deterioration in the quality of land, including its soil, vegetation, and the water systems that depend on it, to a point where the land can no longer perform its ecological or productive functions effectively. It is one of the most widespread environmental challenges of our time, affecting an estimated 3.2 billion people globally.

SDG 15 specifically targets land degradation under Goal 15.3, which commits to achieving land degradation neutrality meaning that any new land degradation must be balanced or outweighed by land restoration elsewhere. Despite this commitment, progress has been slow and degradation continues at an alarming rate.

Land degradation is often described as a 'slow disaster' it lacks the dramatic visibility of a flood or wildfire, but its cumulative effects are equally devastating to food security, biodiversity, and human wellbeing.
